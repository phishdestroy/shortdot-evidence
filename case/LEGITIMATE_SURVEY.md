## The Challenge

ShortDot SA describes its zones as home to "businesses, creators, and communities around the world."

We test this claim with data. We are actively looking for counterexamples. The methodology:

**A domain qualifies as LEGITIMATE if ALL of the following are true:**

1. ✅ Verifiable company or organization registration in any jurisdiction
2. ✅ Clear, lawful business purpose (not just a landing page or redirect)
3. ✅ Not impersonating another brand
4. ✅ Not listed in Spamhaus DBL, SURBL, URLhaus, or ThreatFox
5. ✅ Serves actual content as its primary or official domain (not a secondary experiment)

**Burden of proof:** submission must include evidence — company registration number, clear business description, and confirmation the ShortDot TLD domain is used as primary/official presence (not just registered defensively).

## Rejected Submissions

Submissions are rejected if:

- The domain is registered defensively (brand blocking, not operational)
- The domain redirects to a .com or other primary domain
- The "business" cannot be verified against public company registries
- The domain appears in any abuse intelligence feed
- The domain was registered after this investigation published (post-registration gaming)

## How to Submit

Open a GitHub issue titled: `Legitimate site: [domain]`

Include:
1. The domain name
2. Business name and description
3. Company registration number + jurisdiction
4. Why you believe this passes all 5 legitimacy criteria
5. Evidence URL (company registry, LinkedIn, etc.)

All submissions are reviewed within 48 hours. Verified legitimate sites are added to this table and counted in the live stats.

## Context: Why This Matters

If these zones genuinely served "businesses, creators, and communities," we would expect:
- Thousands of verifiable legitimate businesses
- Global brand recognition among users of .icu, .sbs, .bond addresses
- A meaningful non-abuse registration base in each zone

The .com zone has ~160 million domains. Among these, the overwhelming majority represent genuine registrant intent (even if only parking or future plans). Among ShortDot zones, the ratio of confirmed abuse to confirmed legitimate use is, based on available data, not comparable to any established TLD.

This survey exists to measure that ratio precisely. Open it. Prove us wrong.
